Biography

Born in Iperu-Remo in 1965, into the family of Salami and Yaya-Badmus, his early life was rooted in discipline, ambition, and strong cultural values.

He began his educational journey at Ans-Undeen Primary School, continued at Ojota Secondary School (formerly Nigeria College), and later completed his secondary education at Christ Apostolic Secondary School, Iperu-Remo. He was the first commercial student given the role of Head Boy of his high school due to his intellect and leadership skills.

His academic pursuit led him to Ilaro Polytechnic, where he further honed his skills and drive.

His professional career began at Eko Hotel Holiday Inn, where he served as Room Service Assistant Manager. It was here that he discovered a vision that would propel him toward global experiences and entrepreneurial pursuits.

With an adventurous spirit and unwavering determination, Yaya-Badmus embarked on a journey that took him across various countries including Italy, Malta, Libya, the UAE, Germany, Saudi Arabia, and more. Each stop enriched his worldview and fueled his passion for business and innovation.

In the early 2000s, he set his sights on Ghana, determined to build a legacy in real estate. He migrated to Accra with a clear mission — and today, that vision stands tall. He is the proud CEO of Royal Jama Real Estate, a successful company that continues to grow and make impact in the Ghanaian property market.

Beyond his business success, Yaya-Badmus is deeply rooted in culture and tradition. He holds respected chieftaincy titles, including the Agbakin of Idena-Kingdom, a testament to his leadership and contributions to his community.
